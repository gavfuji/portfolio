The Wing

Overview

The Wing is a 1000mm span, sub-250g flying wing park flyer. This aircraft features a fully foam structure and a more powerful motor for more advanced piloting.

The Wing is still a work in progress, with planned changes to improve handling and structural integrity.

Design

The goal of The Wing was to design a lightweight, fun-to-fly aircraft using a single foam board as the primary construction material. While the aircraft has fewer parts than other designs (see The Stick), the flying wing configuration presents unique design challenges, particularly in stability and control. The aircraft uses elevons for pitch and roll control, with endplates for yaw stability and a moderate sweep to shift the center of gravity backwards to improve handling characteristics. The wings feature a KFm-2 airfoil from the root to improve lift and stall characteristics, transitioning to a flat plate airfoil at the tips to simplify construction and reduce weight. The mount is 3D-printed to ensure the motor remains secure during flight.

Wing area was determined by using the lift equation and a stall speed of ~5 m/s at the maximum allowable weight of 250g. Endplate and control surface sizing sizing was approximated as a percentage of the wing area based on existing designs and adjusted through flight testing. The motor and propeller were selected to maximize thrust without overheating.

Specifications

  • Wingspan: 1000mm
  • Length: 350mm
  • Weight: 235g (with battery)
  • Motor: 2305 2800kV
  • ESC: 40A
  • Propeller: 6x4E
  • Battery: 2S 650mAh LiPo
  • Stall Speed: 4.0 m/s
  • Cruise Speed: 7.5 m/s
  • Max Speed (burst): 21.3 m/s
  • Flight Time: ~8 minutes

Future Plans

Since this was my first flying wing design, there were several issues that I plan to address in future iterations of the aircraft. Although the wing was reinforced spanwise using unidirectional tape, the foam did not hold up well under lift forces and after crashes. I plan to redesign the aircraft using a smaller aspect ratio to improve structural integrity and make the aircraft easier to launch by hand. For similar reasons, the control surfaces were less effective than originally estimated. The foam warped mid-flight to counteract the movement of the elevons. The redesign will include larger control surfaces and a more rigid construction.
