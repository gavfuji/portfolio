About Me

Let's start with the important stuff. No-frills, excuses, or lofty promises. I give my everything to the things that matter to me and look at the long-term to shape the short-term.

This means three things:

  1. I'm accountable.

    If I make a mistake, I'm always ready to take the fault. It is by learning from mistakes that I improve myself and become more valuable as a person.

  2. I'm reliable.

    I don't do things without knowing what's in it for me. That doesn't mean fame, wealth, or other material goods, but unique experiences or opportunities to learn and grow. This means that if I commit to a task, you can count on me to follow through because I know I have something to gain from it.

  3. I'm communicative.

    If I see a problem, I will bring it up even if I caused it (see "accountable"). I'm not afraid to admit I'm wrong or confused because staying silent doesn't make me any less wrong or confused. I'm not afraid to discuss issues because I undertand the success of the team is independent of our emotions. I've seen the difference between good and bad communication and I know which one causes a lot fewer headaches for everyone involved.

I'm Gavin,

a 4th year mechanical and aerospace engineering student at the University of California, Irvine. I want to make a difference without relying on anything other than what I bring to the table.

Over the past few years, I've held a few roles I'm grateful to have taken on. These include:

  • Project Manager for Design/Build/Fly at UCI
  • Finance Director for the American Society of Engineering at UCI
  • Teaching Assistant for the UC California State Summer School for Mathematics & Science

In each of these roles, I learned something new about the world of engineering, whether it be about leadership and management, event organization and finances, or inspiring the next generation of promising talent. Such experiences motivate me to overcome the toughest challenges and seek out opportunities to be a part of something unforgettable.

In my free time, I love trying new things. A recent hobby I've picked up is building and flying RC aircraft with a local soaring club. Otherwise, I'm typically out at the gym, hanging out with my friends, or getting caught up in a "side" project that's way over my head (e.g. this website).
