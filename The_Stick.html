The Stick

Overview

The Stick is a simple, easy-to-fly sub-250g park flyer based on the Slow Stick, a popular choice for beginner RC pilots. This aircraft features a high wing area and large control surfaces to fly steadily at low speeds and a basic foam board construction for easy repairs and modifications.

Design

The central structural component of The Stick is a 6mm carbon fiber tube that runs from the nose to the tail, providing rigidity and strength while minimizing weight. The wings and tail surfaces are constructed from lightweight foam board, which is both easy to cut and shape as well as being inexpensive and readily available. The wing uses a stepped airfoil design to improve lift and slow-speed handling characteristics and a slight dihedral for roll stability. The 3D-printed parts connect all of the major components together, including the wing, tails, motor mount, and other electronics. They rely on low-tolerance friction fits to both remain secure during flight and be easily removed for transportation and rapid design changes. The parts were designed in SolidWorks and printed using lightweight PLA filament on a 3D-printer.

Wing area was determined by using the lift equation and a stall speed of ~5 m/s at the maximum weight of 250g. Tail sizing was performed using standard tail volume coefficients for a similar aircraft. The 1806 2300 kV motor and 8 x 4.1 propeller were selected to achieve a target cruise speed of ~10 m/s while meeting weight requirements.

Specifications

  • Wingspan: 840mm
  • Length: 670mm
  • Weight: 240g (with battery)
  • Motor: 1806 2300kV
  • ESC: 20A
  • Propeller: 8x4.1SF
  • Battery: 2S 650mAh LiPo
  • Stall Speed: 4.8 m/s
  • Cruise Speed: 8.6 m/s
  • Max Speed (burst): 24.6 m/s
  • Flight Time: ~7 minutes
